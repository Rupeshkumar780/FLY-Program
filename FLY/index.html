⨀ Five Pillars to Find the Leader in YOU

Taking Initiative:

• to be the first one to do something, noticing opportunities and taking action especially to solve a problem.

• Taking initiative pays off. It is hard to visualize someone as a leader if he is always waiting to be told what to do by someone else.

• It can be challenging to step out of your comfort zone and try something new, but without taking risks, you limit your potential for success.

• taking initiative : taking calculated Risks and practicing Risk Mitigation Strategies.

Innovativeness:

• Innovativeness is the practical implementation of ideas that result in the introduction of new goods or services.

• Avoiding "Chalta hai" Mindset.

• Overcoming Assumptive Limitations and Associative Barriers by "SCAMPLER Technique" and "Kaizen philosophy".

• Out-of-the-box thinking involving discarding conventional problem-solving methods to truly understand user's problems, challenge old assumptions and foster innovation.

Perseverence:

• It is the continued effort to do or achieve something despite difficulties, setbacks, failures, and obstacles.

• I can try new things no matter how hard they seem. I will not give up. I can do my best. I will keep trying even when I feel like I have failed. I will believe in myself. I can persevere.

• To persevere: Identify your top-level goal and try to find out the main-aspects of the top-level goal.

• Pyramid Goal Strategy : Structured approach for setting and achieving goals by breaking them down into long-term, mid-term, and short-term levels.

Conscientiousness:

• To be Conscientious, you must - 

·Know what is right.
·Develop the will to do the right thing. 
·Address internal conflicts about what to do.

• Conscientiousness: The quality that makes you answer yes to the question "Am I guilty even if I'm not caught?"

• Goals are meant to be achieved, Not by day-dreaming but by Conscientiousness.

• Personal Code of Conduct : the set of behaviour rules you live by, guiding every action you take in life.

Problem Solving:

• Identify the real problem - not the symptom.

• It's the act of defining a problem; determining the root cause of the problem; identifying, prioritizing, and finding a implementable solution.

• 5-Why's Technique: first identify the causes at the first level, and then ask why again and again until to reach to the root cause, and that which has an implementable solution.

• Reverse Brainstorming : Creative problem-solving technique that involves Reversing the problem to generate Solutions.
